Planit — AI-Powered Planning Application

Founder & Product Lead

Jan 2025 – Present

Overview

Planit is an AI-driven productivity tool designed to integrate seamlessly with Gmail and Google Calendar. It automatically ingests emails, identifies actionable items, and generates personalized schedules. The project began from a personal need to cut down on time spent triaging messages and planning daily tasks.

My Role

I led the product definition and end-to-end development. I scoped the MVP through PRD-style documentation, defined success metrics (e.g., % of plans accepted as-is, task acceptance rate, conflict resolution accuracy), and built the product roadmap with a prioritization framework (RICE).

Key Contributions

  • Problem Definition & Scoping: Identified the core user problem (manual triage and scheduling) and narrowed the MVP to three features: focused inbox, NLP-driven task extraction, and conflict-aware scheduling.
  • Product Design Decisions: Balanced feasibility and user value when prioritizing integrations with Gmail/Calendar over more complex Slack/Teams ingestion. Chose metrics-first design (success measured by reduced manual edits per plan).
  • Technical Execution: Built the system using Next.js, React, Prisma, PostgreSQL, Redis, and GPT-4, handling OAuth flows, rate limits, and timezone edge cases.
  • Iteration Framework: Set up experimentation infrastructure (multi-armed bandit testing, signals tracking, semantic memory) to guide future optimization and personalization.
  • Future Roadmap: Plans include pilot testing with student/professional users, refining onboarding to reduce setup friction, and adding analytics dashboards for productivity insights.
